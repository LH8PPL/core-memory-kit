**Core rule**: Do not save research notes when a topic brought has nothing to do with the kit.

**Refinement** (D-158): Always deliver verdict in chat (proof of assessment), and only write a memo when a future session would need it. Write-worthy reasons:
- A borrowed idea applicable to the kit
- A non-obvious rejection (clarifies why we won't use something)
- A reaffirmation of a standing decision under revisit

**Default**: orthogonal-and-obvious → chat only, no file.

**Why:** Keeps research archive focused and load-bearing; prevents cluttering with unrelated material; ensures every saved memo is something a future session will actually use

**How to apply:** Before writing a research note, ask "Will the next session need this?" For unrelated topics with no load-bearing answer, deliver the verdict verbally and move on.
