**Phase 1 (Enumerate)**: Pull actual population from primary sources—two survey references, procedural-memory cohort, fresh arXiv/web sweep, unvisited cites. Avoids convenience sampling.

**Phase 2 (Triage)**: Cheap README/abstract pass; dedupe against already-evaluated systems; kill obvious non-starters but COUNT in denominator (no silent dropping).

**Phase 3 (DeepRead)**: Code/paper-level on ~24 promising candidates; hunt for novel signal types not in current portfolio; judge oracle-dependence (session-transferable vs benchmark-gated).

**Phase 4 (Synthesize)**: Report honest X-of-N field coverage, oracle-free distribution, signal saturation, whether prior claims ("memclaw ~unique", "3 of 9") survive contact with full population.

Scope: ~30–50 systems (vs. prior 11).

**Why:** Convenience sampling masked blind spots; systematic enumeration grounds representativeness claims in actual field coverage, not selection bias.

**How to apply:** Apply this protocol when claiming findings are field-representative; always enumerate denominator before reporting fractions.
